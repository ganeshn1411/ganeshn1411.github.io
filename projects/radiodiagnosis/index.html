Automation of Radio Diagnosis

Paper



Problem

While the requirement for radiologists is increasing day by day, there is a huge supply demand gap due to the dearth of radiologists. In addition, there are discrepencies in diagnosis among various doctors. An automated system to detect tumor cells can be a tool to assist doctors in faster cancer identification enabling them to cater to wider population.

Solution

Supervised Learning techniques to classify a given cell as a cancer cell or not can be used to identify cancer cells. The solution presented here uses Support Vector Machines to achieve the classification.

Data

The data involved labelled set of 500 images obtained from MRI scans of brain. 90 out of the 500 images actually had a tumor.

Results

The data was randomized and split into training, cross-validation and test sets in 70:20:10 ratio. Different Classification algorithms like Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Naive Bayes and Support Vector Machines were tried. RBF Kernel Support Vactor Machines perfomed the best giving an accuracy of about 90% on the test dataset.
