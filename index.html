About Me

My Story

I started my journey into Computer Science in my undergrad. I was instantly attracted to the concept of taking math and logic and applying it to solve problems. The feeling when you build something was always magical and what kept me going.

When I was in my third year, I was introduced to Data Mining. The concept of using data and simple concepts like association rules and decision trees to build systems that were smarter sounded fascinating. After graduating from my bachelor's program, I went on to work at Cisco as an Software Engineer. Here, I was exposed to world of IoT(Internet of Things) and how data analytics on the data from these sensors can build smart cities. I was convinced that the field of Data Science was the future of computer science and wanted to build my expertise on it. That's when I decided to do my Masters in Information Systems focused on Data Analytics at Carnegie Mellon University.

Since graduating from my Masters', I have been working at Mastercard as a Data Scientist. I am part of the rapid prototyping team within the Data Analytics division involved in figuring out the right technologies and data to solve a business need of our client and building prototypes for the same. I am also involved in identifying opportunities within the company to bring in Artificial Intelligence into the workforce by building AI products and educating teams on how to apply it in their world.

Outside my work, I like to spend my time playing mandolin, swimming, reading books or the de-facto fallback of watching Netflix.
